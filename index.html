Gradient Descent as a Dynamical System: Stability, Phase Transitions & Convergence

Treats each gradient update step as a discrete-time map Φ_η : ℝⁿ → ℝⁿ and derives exact stability conditions via spectral radius analysis. Characterises three regimes — geometric convergence, oscillatory divergence, explosion — with interactive bifurcation diagrams and theory-vs-empirical verification.

Transformer Theory Interactive · 5 Live Figures

Spectral Properties of Attention Matrices in Transformer Models

Investigates eigenvalue concentration, power-law singular value decay (σᵢ ≈ C/i^α, α ∈ [0.5, 1.0]), norm scaling, and the sparsity–spectrum duality in transformer attention. Derives expressivity bounds and practical sparsity guidelines from first principles.
